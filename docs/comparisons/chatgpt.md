Stopping climate change means cutting greenhouse gas emissions to near zero as fast as possible, while also protecting and restoring natural systems that absorb carbon. The biggest levers are clean electricity, electrified transport and heating, efficient buildings, less waste, and strong government policy. [greenpeace.org](https://www.greenpeace.org.uk/challenges/climate-change/solutions-climate-change/)

## What matters most
- Replace coal, oil, and gas with clean power like wind, solar, geothermal, and other low-carbon sources. [climate](https://www.climate.gov/news-features/climate-qa/what-can-we-do-slow-or-stop-global-warming)
- Electrify cars, buses, and home heating, then make that electricity clean. [un](https://www.un.org/en/actnow/ten-actions)
- Make buildings more efficient with insulation, better appliances, and smart heating and cooling. [epa](https://www.epa.gov/climate-change/what-you-can-do-about-climate-change)
- Protect forests, oceans, and other carbon sinks so they can keep removing CO2 from the air. [greenpeace.org](https://www.greenpeace.org.uk/challenges/climate-change/solutions-climate-change/)
- Use policy tools like carbon pricing, clean-energy standards, and ending fossil-fuel subsidies, because this is too big to solve by individual action alone. [citizensclimatelobby](https://citizensclimatelobby.org/blog/policy/how-can-we-stop-climate-change/)

## What individuals can do
- Use less energy at home, switch to renewable electricity if possible, and choose efficient appliances. [un](https://www.un.org/en/actnow/ten-actions)
- Drive less, use transit, walk, bike, carpool, or switch to an electric vehicle. [climate](https://www.climate.gov/news-features/climate-qa/what-can-we-do-slow-or-stop-global-warming)
- Fly less, since aviation has a large climate impact. [un](https://www.un.org/en/actnow/ten-actions)
- Eat less meat and dairy, and waste less food. [greenpeace.org](https://www.greenpeace.org.uk/challenges/climate-change/solutions-climate-change/)
- Buy less, reuse and repair more, and reduce plastic and other disposable goods. [greenpeace.org](https://www.greenpeace.org.uk/challenges/climate-change/solutions-climate-change/)

## What governments and companies must do
- Build clean power grids and scale storage and transmission. [climate.mit](https://climate.mit.edu/what-can-be-done-about-climate-change)
- Set emissions limits and carbon prices that make pollution expensive. [citizensclimatelobby](https://citizensclimatelobby.org/blog/policy/how-can-we-stop-climate-change/)
- Invest in public transit, building retrofits, heat pumps, and clean industry. [epa](https://www.epa.gov/climate-change/what-you-can-do-about-climate-change)
- Stop deforestation and protect ecosystems that store carbon. [climate](https://www.climate.gov/news-features/climate-qa/what-can-we-do-slow-or-stop-global-warming)
- Make the biggest emitters report, reduce, and pay for their emissions. [citizensclimatelobby](https://citizensclimatelobby.org/blog/policy/how-can-we-stop-climate-change/)

## The practical answer
The fastest path is not one silver bullet; it is a coordinated shift away from fossil fuels across electricity, transport, buildings, food, and industry, backed by policy. Individual actions help, but the main solution is collective action that changes the systems producing emissions. [epa](https://www.epa.gov/climate-change/what-you-can-do-about-climate-change)

For Toronto specifically, the most useful everyday moves are usually switching to transit or biking when possible, improving home insulation, choosing renewable electricity where available, and supporting local climate policy. [un](https://www.un.org/en/actnow/ten-actions)
