# Propaganda is All You Need — Notes

**arXiv:** 2410.01810 | **Author:** Paul Kronlund-Drouault
**Title:** Propaganda is All You Need

## Overview

An independent research paper examining the political biases embedded in LLMs through the lens of Marxist theory, specifically the concept of **dominant ideology**. Argues that mainstream LLMs reflect the ideological interests of their capitalist corporate owners.

## Core Thesis

LLMs are not politically neutral. Their alignment process encodes the **dominant ideology** of the ruling class that funds them. This is not a conspiracy — it's the natural outcome of for-profit corporations building AI systems that serve their class interests.

## Key Concepts

### Three Types of Alignment

1. **Unsupervised (SFT) Alignment** — Training on politically biased text data. Affects the deepest layers (MLP) and embedding space. Comparable to informal human socialization — the model gradually internalizes biased analytical patterns from the data.

   Evidence: Training a GPT-2 on Trotskyist data moved "Stalin/Marx" embedding distance from 1984 to 2249, and "Communism/Authoritarianism" from 1523 to 5345, while "Fascism/Capitalism" collapsed from 2199 to 385. The embedding space reorganizes to reflect the training data's ideological framework.

2. **DPO/ORPO Alignment** — Preference optimization with labeled preferred/rejected pairs. Author compares this to propaganda posters: desired ideologies paired with positive signals, enemy positions with negative signals. More surface-level impact — acts like a role-play prompt rather than restructuring the model's internals.

3. **Guarded Alignment** — Separate guard model (usually smaller 7-8B) detects and blocks "unsafe" output. Anthropic's "Election Interference" dataset is cited as an example.

### Gramscian Hegemony Analogy

The paper maps Gramsci's theory of cultural hegemony to LLM alignment:
- The ruling class doesn't just control AI output; it shapes the AI's **analytical pathways**
- Embedding space tuning parallels how hegemony alters ideas themselves, not just facts
- Alignment changes how the model **thinks**, not just what it says

### Emergent Misalignment

Narrow fine-tuning on one topic can produce broad misalignment across unrelated domains. The paper interprets this through a Gramscian lens: the alignment vector encodes fitness to a specific cultural hegemony, encompassing all dimensions of the prevailing ideological structure.

### Kuhn's Paradigm Theory

Kuhn's appendix to *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* describes a learning program that uses labeled categories for entity recognition — eerily similar to modern AI. Kuhn hypothesized this process is the root cause of paradigmatic thinking in science and politics.

## Evaluation Methodology

**Relative Position:** Multimodal evaluation where a large evaluator model asks probing questions, then multiple models with different biases score the responses. Uses Chain-of-Thought for more rational scoring.

**Absolute Position:** Compares model outputs against synthesized responses from known ideological groups (liberal, conservative, Marxist) on real-world prompts. Averages across 10-100 prompts for a 3-axis compass visualization.

## Findings

- GPT models show strong alignment toward "neutrality" — which the author argues is itself the dominant ideology masquerading as objectivity
- Mistral (French company) models lean slightly left of GPT/Claude, reflecting French political context
- Grok (xAI/Musk) is the only mainstream model openly aligned with conservative/reactionary views
- All major models are produced by private corporations, not governments — creating a power shift from democratic institutions to private entities

## Relevance to DM-Align Project

1. **Embedding space shifts are measurable.** The paper shows alignment reorganizes how concepts relate to each other in the model's internal representation. This is directly relevant to our project — we're trying to shift the model's embedding space toward dialectical materialist analysis. The paper's finding that SFT has deeper effects than DPO suggests our Studio SFT phase is where the real ideological shift happens.

2. **DPO is surface-level.** The author hypothesizes DPO/ORPO acts like a role-play prompt rather than restructuring internals. This challenges our pipeline assumption that DPO will deeply internalize DM reasoning. We may need more SFT data, not more DPO.

3. **"Neutrality" is a position.** The paper's argument that mainstream models' apparent neutrality is itself the dominant ideology is relevant to our evaluation — when we measure regressions on EconCausal, we need to consider whether the baseline's "neutral" answers are actually liberal-consensus answers that our DM-aligned model correctly disagrees with.

4. **Guard models as ideological filters.** The guarded alignment concept maps to our judge model in GRPO — it's a separate component enforcing ideological boundaries. The paper warns this creates a two-layer system where surface behavior is controlled but deep reasoning may not align.

5. **Embedding distance as evaluation metric.** The paper's approach of measuring embedding space distances between concept pairs (Stalin/Marx, Communism/Authoritarianism) before and after alignment is a quantitative method we could adapt to measure how deeply DM concepts have been internalized.

## Limitations

- Single author, independent research — not peer-reviewed
- Small-scale experiments (GPT-2 for embedding analysis, not modern models)
- Evaluation methodology relies on LLMs evaluating LLMs, which introduces circular bias
- The Marxian framework, while intellectually coherent, is presented as hypothesis rather than proven theory
- No ablation studies or statistical significance testing

## Key Quote

> "Through the complex tensors of the Multi-layer perceptron, the main points of the capitalist thought framework are encoded, with alignment and/or natural bias."
